How to Play Empire Warriors

Game Overview

Empire Warriors is a deck building card game featuring warriors and weapons from three historical empires: Sikh, British, and Mughal. Build your forces, equip them with weapons, and defeat your opponent!

Factions

  • Sikh Empire: Warriors of faith with unique weapons like the Chakkar and Khanda. Strong cavalry and elite warriors.
  • British Empire: Colonial forces with advanced firearms and disciplined infantry. Strong ranged capabilities.
  • Mughal Empire: Imperial warriors with ornate weapons and diverse troops. Strong administrative and tactical advantages.

Card Types

  • Warriors: Play these cards to your field. Each warrior has a power value:
    • Nihang Singh: Elite Sikh warrior (Power: 3)
    • Akali Nihang: Fearless warrior of the Akali Nihang order (Power: 4)
    • Gatka Master: Expert in the Sikh martial art (Power: 2)
    • Panj Pyare: The Five Beloved Ones (Power: 6)
    • Sikh Cavalry: Mounted warrior (Power: 5)
    • Misal Sardar: Leader of a Sikh confederacy (Power: 4)
    • Shaheed Singh: Martyr warrior (Power: 3)
    • Akali Warrior: Immortal warrior (Power: 3)
    • Gursikh Warrior: Devoted follower of the Guru's path (Power: 2)
  • Weapons: Equip these to warriors to increase their power:
    • Kirpan: Ceremonial sword (+2 power)
    • Khanda: Double-edged sword (+3 power)
    • Chakkar: Throwing weapon (+1 power)
    • Nagni Barcha: Long spear with wavy blade (+4 power)
    • Dhal Shield: Traditional shield (+1 power)
    • Teer Kaman: Bow and arrows (+2 power)
    • Bhala: Long spear (+3 power)

Game Mechanics

  1. Energy: You have 5 energy per turn to play cards.
  2. Playing Cards: Click on warrior cards to play them directly to your field.
  3. Equipping Weapons: Click on a weapon card, then click on a warrior to equip it. You can click the weapon again to unselect it if you change your mind.
  4. Weapon Indicators: Equipped weapons are shown as small icons on the warrior card.
  5. Recalling Cards: Click the recall button (↩) on a field card to return it to your hand and refund its energy cost.
  6. Combat: At the end of each turn, the side with higher total power wins and earns points.
  7. Turn Limit: The game lasts for 10 turns. The player with the highest score wins.

Controls

  • End Turn: Resolve combat, discard your hand, and draw new cards.
  • New Game: Reset the game and start over.
